Who This Backend Serves

Two users, two different reasons for wanting better sequencing.

These profiles explain who will use the platform, what they want from it, and how the backend should support their playlist analysis workflow.

User 1

Beginner DJ

DJ
Role of the user

A beginner DJ who wants the tool to explain which songs mix well together and which songs do not based on musical features.

Goals of the user
  • Understand which track pairs are good candidates for smooth DJ mixing.
  • See why certain songs may not mix well because of mismatched tempo, key, energy, or other audio features.
  • Use the suggestions to make better decisions while preparing a set.
Tasks of the user for the app
  • Log in with Spotify and choose a playlist or group of songs to evaluate.
  • Send playlist data to the backend so each possible song transition can be scored.
  • Review mixing suggestions that identify which songs mix well and which songs should be avoided together.
  • Use the feature explanations to understand whether tempo, key, energy, or loudness is causing a strong or weak transition.

User 2

Regular Music Listener

ML
Role of the user

A regular music listener who uses the tool for playlist sequencing by submitting a playlist and receiving the best order for that playlist.

Goals of the user
  • Input an existing playlist and get back a better sequence automatically.
  • Enjoy smoother song-to-song flow without needing to understand the technical audio features.
  • Use the reordered playlist for everyday listening, workouts, studying, driving, or parties.
Tasks of the user for the app
  • Log in with Spotify and select the playlist they want sequenced.
  • Submit the playlist to the backend for transition scoring and ordering.
  • Receive the best sequence for the playlist based on overall flow.
  • Listen to the reordered playlist without needing to review detailed mixing explanations.
